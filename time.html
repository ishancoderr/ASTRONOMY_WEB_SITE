Calendar year and Solar year

  • Calendar year/ Civil year – 365 or 366 days

  • Solar/ Tropical year – Interval between successive vernal equinox

Day is the interval between successive upper/lower transits of some celestial body

Celestial bodies have unique movements which create different type of days











Sidereal day/time

  • Sidereal day – Time interval between successive upper transit of the first point of Aries. Day is of uniform length.

  • Sidereal time – Hour angle of the first point of Aries. Time is local because it is related to the local meridian of the observer.

If the first point of Aries is at the upper transit of the observer’s meridian, sidereal time is 0h

Solar day/time

  • The apparent solar day is the time interval between successive lower transit of the Sun.

  • Solar day begins at mid night.







Solar day/time

Solar day varies due to :

  • Kepler’s law – the movement of the Sun is not uniform.

  • The Sun moves along the ecliptic but not equator.

To overcome this problem, a Mean Sun is introduced, which is an imagined to move along the equator with constant angular velocity.

  • Apparent Sun is moving along the ecliptic with non-uniform velocity.

  • Mean Sun is moving along the equator with uniform angular velocity.





Sidereal day/time


Mean Solar Day

  • The average length of all solar days taken from one Vernal equinox to the next, and is taken on lower transit.

Mean Solar Time

  • The hour angle of the mean Sun, taken from the lower transit.













Universal Time (UT/GMT)


UT/GMT is hour angle measured from the lower transit of the Mean Sun from Greenwich meridian.


  • Apparent Sun (or its projection) and the mean Sun are two different objects.
  • They are together 4 times a year
    • Two Equinoxes
    • Two Solstices
    • Equation of time – The angle between apparent and mean Sun.
